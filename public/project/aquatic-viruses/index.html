PhD Project

Feb 14, 2019
Slides Follow

This is my PhD project funded by Region Rhône-Alpes during year 2010 - 2013, working in INRA Thonon-les-bains under the supervision of Dr. Stéphan Jacquet. During three and half years, I conducted research to investigate and highlight the diversity, distribution, dynamic, and community structure of phytoplankton viruses and their functional roles in three large and deep European peri-alpine lakes: Lakes Annecy, Bourget and Geneva. This PhD work increased and enriched our knowledge in freshwater viral ecology and was published as articles in 11 peer-reviewed journals and 2 book chapters: Applied Environmental Microbiology, Environmental Microbiology, Freshwater Biology, FEMS Microbiology Ecology, Hydrology and Earth System Sciences, Microbial Ecology, etc. With such volume of work, Kevin Xu Zhong’s PhD thesis titled “Freshwater dsDNA viral diversity: A special emphasis on viruses infecting phytoplankton (cyanophages and phycodnaviruses) and T4-like myophages in peri-alpine lakes” was selected as the best European PhD Dissertation in Freshwater Sciences during year 2013-2014 by European Federation for Freshwater Sciences. Kevin obtained in 2014 the Chinese Government Award for Outstanding PhD Students Abroad by China Scholarship Council, and in 2015 represented French Society of Limnology won the EFFS Award for the best European PhD Dissertation in Freshwater Sciences during year 2013-2014.
